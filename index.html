Course Modules ↑ Top

Module 1: Introduction to NLP

Overview of NLP, fundamental concepts, and setting up your development environment.

  • Lesson 1.1: What is NLP?
  • Lesson 1.2: Key Concepts and Terminology
  • Lesson 1.3: Tools and Setup (e.g., NLTK, spaCy)

Module 2: Text Preprocessing & Representation

Techniques for cleaning and preparing text data, and different ways to represent text for machine learning.

  • Lesson 2.1: Tokenization, Stemming, Lemmatization
  • Lesson 2.2: Bag-of-Words, TF-IDF
  • Lesson 2.3: Word Embeddings (Word2Vec, GloVe)

Module 3: Sequence Models & Deep Learning for NLP

Introduction to models that handle sequential data, including recurrent neural networks and transformers.

  • Lesson 3.1: Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs)
  • Lesson 3.2: LSTMs and GRUs
  • Lesson 3.3: Transformers and Attention Mechanisms

Module 4: NLP Applications & Advanced Topics

Practical applications of NLP and a look into current research areas.

  • Lesson 4.1: Sentiment Analysis & Text Classification
  • Lesson 4.2: Named Entity Recognition & Question Answering
  • Lesson 4.3: Generative Models (e.g., GPT) & Ethics in NLP

Course Projects ↑ Top

The course project is a significant component of this course (50%), providing an opportunity to apply the concepts and techniques learned to a real-world NLP problem. Students will work individually or in small groups (TBD) to propose, develop, and present a project.

  • Project Proposal: A brief document outlining your project idea, goals, dataset, and methodology.
  • Mid-term Check-in: A short presentation or report on your progress.
  • Final Project Report: A comprehensive write-up detailing your project, experiments, results, and conclusions.
  • Final Presentation/Demo: A presentation or demonstration of your project to the class.

Further details, including specific deadlines, suggested project topics, and evaluation criteria, will be announced on the course platform. We encourage students to think creatively and explore areas of NLP that genuinely interest them.

Assignments and Project Policies ↑ Top

Academic Integrity & Plagiarism

All work submitted in this course must be your own. Plagiarism, including copying code or text from others (students or online sources) without proper attribution, is strictly prohibited and will result in severe penalties, including a failing grade for the assignment/project and potential disciplinary action as per university policy. Familiarize yourself with the university's academic integrity guidelines.

Late Submission Policy

Assignments are due by the specified date and time. Late submissions will be penalized. You will typically have a grace period of [e.g., 24 hours] with a [e.g., 10%] deduction. Submissions beyond this grace period will generally not be accepted, except in cases of documented emergencies or prior arrangements with the instructor.

Collaboration Policy

Collaboration on assignments is encouraged for discussion of concepts and general approaches, but all submitted code and written answers must be your individual work. For projects, group collaboration is expected, and the contribution of each member should be clearly documented. Any specific collaboration guidelines for individual assignments or projects will be provided with the assignment description.

Consultation and Research Opportunities

Students are highly encouraged to consult with the instructor and teaching assistants during office hours or by appointment for guidance on assignments, projects, or course topics. If a project developed in this course shows significant potential for a research paper, students may, at their discretion, invite a TA or the instructor whose advice substantially benefited the work to be a co-author. This is not a requirement, but an opportunity to acknowledge significant contributions and foster academic collaboration.
